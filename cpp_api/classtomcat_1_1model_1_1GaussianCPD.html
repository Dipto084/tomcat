class tomcat::model::GaussianCPD : public tomcat::model::CPD¶

A Gaussian CPD consists of a table containing the mean and variance of a Gaussian distribution of the node that is sampled from this CPD given its parents’ assignments. The number of rows is given by the product of the cardinalities of the parent nodes of such node. Each row represents a combination of possible assignments of the parent nodes ordered with respect to the binary basis. The table is represented by a list of Gaussian distributions that contain the mean and the variance in itself.

For instance,

Let A and B be parents of the node C.

A -> C B -> C

Suppose A, B have cardinalities 2, 3 respectively and C is sampled from a normal distribution with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\).

A GaussianCPD for C will be as follows,

|///| C |

\(\mu\) | \(\sigma^2\) | |—————————————–| | A | B |////////////////////////////////////| |—————————————–| | 0 | 0 | \(\mu_00\) | \(\sigma^2_{00}\) | |—————————————–| | 0 | 1 | \(\mu_01\) | \(\sigma^2_{01}\) | |—————————————–| | 0 | 2 | \(\mu_02\) | \(\sigma^2_{02}\) | |—————————————–| | 1 | 0 | \(\mu_10\) | \(\sigma^2_{10}\) | |—————————————–| | 1 | 1 | \(\mu_11\) | \(\sigma^2_{11}\) | |—————————————–| | 1 | 2 | \(\mu_12\) | \(\sigma^2_{12}\) | |—————————————–|
